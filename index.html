Module 01

Benchmark Scorecard

18 behaviours extracted from Aprocina's transcripts, rated by frequency (how often she does it) and impact (its effect on conversion). Use this to score any rep on any call.

What the Transcripts Reveal

Aprocina's calls show a consistent pattern: she frames herself as the onboarding team from the first sentence, collects profile data within 90 seconds, and converts financial questions into dollar-per-hour figures that customers can immediately grasp. Most critically — she never ends a call without a booked next step. The benchmark below codifies these 18 behaviours for team-wide scoring.

Module 03

The Aprocina Call Framework

A stage-by-stage breakdown of how a benchmark call is structured — extracted directly from Aprocina's transcripts and reverse-engineered into a repeatable model for any rep.

STAGE 01 Identity & Frame Setting 0:00 – 0:30
Goal: Establish authority as the onboarding team, not sales. Position this call as a process, not a pitch.
  • State your name and role as "onboarding team"
  • Reference the referral or inquiry source immediately
  • Ask to confirm you're speaking with the right person
  • Use their name within the first 15 seconds
Aprocina's Language"Hi [name] — I'm calling in regards to a referral that [partner] has sent us through. Are you aware of this? I'm part of the onboarding team and I can get [client name] set up today."
STAGE 02 Process Overview (30-Second Roadmap) 0:30 – 1:30
Goal: Map out exactly what happens next in simple, low-effort terms. Remove ambiguity before questions form.
  • Describe the 3-step process in one breath (profile → care plan meeting → verbal HCA confirmation)
  • Position the first step (profile) as quick and painless
  • Pre-empt the service agreement by framing it as easy: "just read over it"
  • Confirm understanding with "Does that all make sense?"
Aprocina's Language"How that works is — I just need to grab a few details and get [name] onboarded. The next step is we book you in for a care plan and budget meeting, just over the phone. They'll ask if you've read the agreement and you're happy to proceed. Once we get that verbal yes, we let MyAgedCare know you've found a provider. Does that all make sense?"
STAGE 03 Profile Collection 1:30 – 4:00
Goal: Gather all required profile information efficiently, treating each data point as a natural conversation, not an interrogation.
  • Name confirmation → phone → email → date of birth
  • Ask about desired services — and listen actively to expand the list
  • Confirm coordinator preference (self-managed vs fully coordinated)
  • Check if they're an authorised representative for the client
  • Obtain MAC client ID / referral code
Aprocina's Language"Will this number be the best number on the account? What's the best email address? What's [client]'s date of birth? And what sort of services were you wanting to use? — domestic assistance, gardening… what about transport, social support, podiatry?"
STAGE 04 Financial Explanation 4:00 – 7:00
Goal: Answer the cost question with clarity and confidence. Always use dollar figures, never raw percentages.
  • Qualify pension status first: "Full pensioner, part pensioner, or self-funded retiree?"
  • State the out-of-pocket dollar figure immediately: "$13.82 per hour"
  • Give a concrete example: "For 2 hours cleaning a fortnight — that's $27.64 a month from you"
  • Reference competitor pricing where relevant to anchor value
  • Connect services to daily living, not abstract package mechanics
Aprocina's Language"Are you a full pensioner, part pensioner, or a self-funded retiree? … Full pensioner. Okay — you're looking at $13.82 out of pocket per hour. So for cleaning twice a fortnight — that's $27 a month from you. And gardening once a fortnight would be the same. Transport actually falls under Independence Living, so that's only 5% — $2 an hour out of pocket for you."
STAGE 05 Friction Resolution (Real-Time) As Needed
Goal: Remove every blocker during the call, not after it. A call that ends with "I'll get back to you on that" is a pipeline loss.
  • Missing referral code → call MAC live: "I'll just pop you on a brief hold"
  • Uncertainty about workers → introduce Care Vicinity marketplace
  • Fear of losing current workers → "You can keep the same person"
  • Confusion about the process → re-walk the 3-step roadmap
  • Price resistance → reframe to dollar savings vs alternative
Aprocina's Language"I'll just pop you on a brief hold — I have a provider's line so they answer straight away. Won't be too long." [On return:] "So I've done the paperwork side while we were waiting — I just need to book you in for the care plan meeting now."
STAGE 06 Assumptive Close & Next Step Booking Final 2 Minutes
Goal: Leave every call with a booked appointment. Not "I'll send you something." A specific date, time, and confirmation.
  • Offer specific time options — not "when are you free?"
  • State the timezone explicitly if relevant
  • Confirm the appointment and repeat it back
  • Explain what will happen next (service agreement email, care plan meeting format)
  • End warmly and with certainty
Aprocina's Language"On [date], I've got either 10:00, 11:30, 2:30, or 4:00 — all in your Queensland times. What works best for you? … Perfect. I'll lock in 10:00. I'll send you the service agreement through now — just have a read before Monday and you're good to go."
Module 04

1-on-1 Coaching Guide

For team leads to use in weekly 1:1s or post-call reviews. Organised by coaching moment, with diagnostic questions and targeted feedback prompts.

🎯
Opening & Identity Framing
What to Listen For
  • Does the rep say "onboarding team" within the first 30 seconds?
  • Do they use the customer's name early?
  • Is their opening statement process-led or question-led?
Coaching Questions
"Walk me through exactly what you said in the first 30 seconds of that call."
"How did you frame your role? Did you say sales, customer service, or onboarding?"
"When did you first use the customer's name?"
Feedback if Coaching Needed
  • Reinforce: "I'm part of the onboarding team" tells the customer this call has a destination — your current opening doesn't.
  • Role-play the first 30 seconds together. Repeat until the framing is automatic.
⏱
Speed to Onboarding
What to Listen For
  • Is "onboard" or "profile" mentioned within 60 seconds?
  • Does the rep spend more than 5 minutes explaining services before collecting data?
  • Does the customer end up leading the conversation's direction?
Coaching Questions
"At what point in that call did you start collecting profile information?"
"What were you trying to achieve in the first 3 minutes?"
"How long did you spend explaining services before you moved to next steps?"
Feedback if Coaching Needed
  • Data point: Aprocina mentions onboarding by 0:25 on most calls. What's your average?
  • The longer you stay in explanation mode, the more permission you give the customer to stay in passive mode.
💰
Financial Communication
What to Listen For
  • Does the rep use percentages or dollar amounts?
  • Do they qualify pension status before giving a cost figure?
  • Do they give a worked example (e.g. "for 2 hours a fortnight, that's $X")?
Coaching Questions
"How did you explain the out-of-pocket cost to the customer?"
"Did you ask about their pension status before quoting a figure?"
"Did you give them a real-world dollar example — like what a fortnight of cleaning costs them?"
Feedback if Coaching Needed
  • 17.5% of $79 is not a number someone can make a decision with. $13.82 is. Always lead with the dollar.
  • Practice: given a service and pension type, quote the out-of-pocket cost in under 10 seconds.
🔧
Real-Time Friction Removal
What to Listen For
  • Does the rep defer blockers to a follow-up call?
  • When MAC contact is needed, do they initiate it live or promise to do it later?
  • Does the customer leave the call without resolution on their main concern?
Coaching Questions
"Were there any unresolved issues at the end of that call?"
"Was there a moment where you could have called MAC live but didn't? What stopped you?"
"What would it have taken to solve that on the call rather than after it?"
Feedback if Coaching Needed
  • Every "I'll follow up on that" is a momentum leak. 40–60% of follow-ups never result in a conversion.
  • Build the confidence and habit of calling MAC, partners, or internal team live on the call.
📅
Closing & Booking
What to Listen For
  • Does the call end with a confirmed booked time?
  • Does the rep offer specific options or ask an open "when are you free?"
  • Is the service agreement mentioned and framed as easy?
Coaching Questions
"How did you close that call? What was the last thing you confirmed?"
"If the customer didn't book, what was the reason — and was there anything you could have done differently?"
"When you offered times, did you give specific options or ask when they're free?"
Feedback if Coaching Needed
  • Specific time options remove the cognitive load of the customer having to check and propose. "I've got 10 or 11:30 on Monday" converts higher than "when works for you?"
  • No booking = no commitment. No commitment = pipeline stall.
🤝
Empathy & Relationship Tone
What to Listen For
  • Does the rep acknowledge frustration or difficult circumstances?
  • Do they validate without dwelling — keeping momentum intact?
  • Do they use the customer's family member names when mentioned?
Coaching Questions
"How did the customer seem emotionally on that call? Did you acknowledge it?"
"Was there a moment of frustration or concern that you could have acknowledged more?"
Feedback if Coaching Needed
  • Aprocina's tone is warm but not drawn out. She validates quickly and redirects: "Wow — how did they approve it without an OT assessment? Anyway, here's what we can do for you."
  • Empathy without direction feels like therapy. Empathy with a next step feels like care.

Module 05

Training Session Script

A facilitated session guide for your weekly team training. Built around Aprocina's patterns with exercises the whole team can run together. Estimated runtime: 60–75 minutes.

01Opening: The Momentum Principle (10 min)10 min▾
Facilitator Says
Open the session by asking the team a single question: "What's the number one reason a customer goes cold between an inquiry and signing?" Let them answer freely for 2–3 minutes. Capture responses on a whiteboard. Common answers will include: too much information, not following up quickly, confusion about the process. Then reframe:
Key Point to Land
"The research — and our own data — shows that momentum is the single most important variable. A customer who takes one small action on a call is 3–5x more likely to complete onboarding than one who leaves the call without doing anything. That's what we're here to build today: the habit of creating momentum in every single call."
Facilitator Notes
Reference the key finding from the performance report: Aprocina initiates onboarding within the first 0–3 minutes. The rest of the team averages 10–18 minutes. Ask: "What do you think that difference does to conversion?" Allow brief discussion, then move to the data section.
02The Six Stages: What a Benchmark Call Looks Like (15 min)15 min▾
Facilitator Says
Walk the team through the six call stages (using Module 03 from this document as your reference). For each stage, give them the benchmark timing, the goal, and one real language example. Don't rush this — the language examples are what make it concrete.
Exact Language to Share — Stage 1
"Hi [name], I'm calling in regards to a referral that [partner] has sent us through. I'm part of the onboarding team and I can get [client] set up today — I just need to ask a few questions."
Exact Language to Share — Stage 6 (Closing)
"On [day], I've got 10:00, 11:30, 2:30, or 4:00 — these are all in your local time. What works for you?"
Facilitator Tip
Emphasise that the difference between Stage 1 and what most reps currently do is often one sentence. The goal is not to overhaul the call — it's to change the first 30 seconds.
03Live Exercise: The 30-Second Open (15 min)15 min▾
Activity
Pair role-play: opening only
Pair up the team. One person plays the rep, one plays the customer. The customer gives one of three scenarios:

(A) "I just got a referral from my doctor's office."
(B) "I filled in a form on your website — just exploring options."
(C) "My son found you on Google and gave me your number."

The rep has 30 seconds only to open the call using the benchmark language. After each attempt, the "customer" gives one piece of feedback: "Did I feel like I was being helped or being sold to?"

Run for 3 rounds per pair, then debrief as a group.
Debrief Questions
  • "Who found it hard to say 'onboarding team' in the opening? Why?"
  • "Did using the client's name feel natural? What made it awkward?"
  • "What was the difference between the attempts that felt like help and the ones that felt like sales?"
04The Dollar Test: Financial Communication Drill (10 min)10 min▾
Facilitator Says
Tell the team: "I'm going to give you a service, a package level, and a pension type. You have 10 seconds to give me the out-of-pocket dollar figure AND a worked example. No percentages." Run through 5–6 rapid-fire scenarios as a group exercise.
Sample Scenarios
Rapid Fire — call on individual reps
1. Level 2 · Full pensioner · Domestic assistance ($79/hr) → "$13.82/hr. Two hours a fortnight = $27.64/month."

2. Level 3 · Full pensioner · Transport (Independence Living, $40/hr) → "$2/hr."

3. Level 4 · Part pensioner · Gardening ($85/hr) → Calculate and express as a dollar figure with an example.

4. Level 2 · Self-funded retiree · Podiatry → "$0 out of pocket."

Correct any percentage-based answers immediately. The rule is simple: dollar amount, then worked example. Nothing else.
Why This Matters
When Aprocina says "$13.82" and the customer says "so for two hours that's about $28?" — that's the customer doing the maths themselves. That mental engagement is a micro-commitment. The customer is no longer passive.
05Objection Clinic: The Top 5 Blockers (15 min)15 min▾
Facilitator Says
Present the five most common blockers observed in the transcript data. For each one, ask the team how they currently handle it, then present the benchmark response.
Blocker 1: "I don't have the referral code with me."
Benchmark: "That's okay — I'll just pop you on a brief hold and call MyAgedCare directly. I have a provider line so they answer straight away."
Blocker 2: "I don't want to lose my current cleaner / physio."
Benchmark: "You can continue using the same person — that's completely fine. They'd just invoice us through the package."
Blocker 3: "I need to read the agreement first — can I call you back?"
Benchmark: "Absolutely — I'll send it through now. Let me book you in for [day], and that gives you a few days to read it before we chat. Does 10am work?"
Blocker 4: "I'm just exploring options — I'm not ready to commit."
Benchmark: "That's completely fine. The profile takes about 5 minutes and it doesn't commit you to anything. It just means everything's ready when you are — which saves a lot of time later."
Blocker 5: "Your fees seem higher than [competitor]."
Benchmark: "Price-wise, you won't find anyone cheaper than us for the management fee — and we work with local independent workers so you're also not paying agency rates for your cleaner or gardener."
06Weekly Commitment & Close (5 min)5 min▾
Facilitator Says
Close by asking each rep to nominate one specific behaviour from today's session that they will apply on every call this week. Write them down. Review the following week — not to police, but to learn from what worked.
Commitment Exercise
Each rep states aloud:
"This week I am going to [specific behaviour] on every call."

Examples of strong commitments:
— "Say 'onboarding team' in my opening on every call."
— "Give a dollar figure with a worked example on every cost question."
— "Book a specific time before I end every substantive call."

Vague commitments ("be better at closing") should be redirected to something observable.
Facilitator Note
The goal of this session is not perfection — it is one changed behaviour per rep per week. In four weeks, that's four new behaviours per rep. In eight weeks, the team is operating noticeably closer to the benchmark.
